Redesigning School’s Student Center

(2023)

Background

Since I was a freshman, I had always found the the school’s one-for-all student information page (named MySJSU) difficult to navigate. Even as a senior, I still find myself struggling to find certain information because the portal is overpopulated with links. There is no visual cue on where to look and MySJSU commits the gravest sin of all: it's very ugly.

Research

While I know I'm not alone in trying to navigate the site, I didn't know exactly what were the pain points that everyone else experienced. I sent out a survey to gather what other students think about MySJSU and how they feel using it. Six students had responded.

A majority of the answers lay in the center of the scale, indicating that most students feel that MYSJSU is satisfactory, but not completely satisfied with it

I polled them on which areas of MYSJSU they frequently used and found that MyProgress, Messages, and MyScheduler are the most commonly used on the page.

In the long answer response, it appears that most students would prefer their MYSJSU page to be more straightforward.

Wireframe/Mockup

When designing a new mockup for MYSJSU, I decided to break the site down into big shapes, so whatever that fell under one umbrella would be in one place.

In Figma, I created more or less the same big shapes for my low fidelity prototypes and expanded upon the colors and layout design.

Result

I simplified the overall design by a lot, keeping everything into specific sections, allowing the eye to scan over information quickly. I removed all the links and words for a cleaner interface.

The search bar was bigger now and right next to the title of the page, so it would be one of the first things the user would look at when the page finished loading.

Conclusion

When processing my research information, I realized that I neglected the other portions of the student center that other users might’ve used in my survey. I made a mistake in limiting the choices to only those five tools.

Another thing I realized was that I would’ve liked to see a first-time user try to interact with the old MYSJSU page because it would’ve shown me the pain points of the old design.
